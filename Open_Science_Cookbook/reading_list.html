TOPS Reading List#

Some recommended open science reading spanning from official policy to publications to educational materials. If you are looking for resources to learn how to code, please check out this list.

  • UNESCO 2021 Recommendations on Open Science

    • Update: The recording and a summary report of the second meeting of the UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science held on 26 September 2022

    • You may find the recordings and reports from all the Working Group meetings on UNESCO’s Open Science webpage

  • NASEM 2018 Open Science by Design

  • NASEM 2018 Best Practices for a Future Open Code Policy for NASA Space Science

  • NASEM 2021 Developing a Toolkit for Fostering Open Science Practices: Proceedings of a Workshop

  • NASEM 2022 Open Scholarship Priorities and Next Steps: Proceedings of a Workshop–in Brief

  • AGU Article “From open data to open science” 2021 article

  • NASA SMD 2021 Scientific Information policy for the Science Mission Directorate

  • CERN Open Science Policy and accompanying resources

  • Gownaris, N.J., Vermeir, K., Bittner, M.-I., Gunawardena, L., Kaur-Ghumaan, S., Lepenies, R., Ntsefong, G.N. and Zakari, I.S., 2022.   • Masuzzo and Martens (2017). Do you speak Open Science? Resources and tips to learn the language. doi.org/10.7287/peerj.preprints.2689v1

  • Resources for Earth Science: https://www.earthdatascience.org/

  • Hills, D. J., Damerow, J. E., Ahmmed, B., Catolico, N., Chakraborty, S., Coward, C. M., et al. (2022). Earth and Space Science Informatics perspectives on Integrated, Coordinated, Open, Networked (ICON) science. Earth and Space Science, 9, e2021EA002108. https://doi.org/10.1029/2021EA002108

    • TTW: https://zenodo.org/record/6841867: Broadening the definition and implementation of accessibility, inclusion and incentivisation in open science

    • IOI response: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.6828389 (also Blog): Community-sourced best practices for investing in and contributing to open infrastructure
